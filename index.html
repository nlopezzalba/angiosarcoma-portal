Help transform our understanding of Angiosarcoma.

If you have or had angiosarcoma, join a nationwide movement of patients, doctors, and scientists by sharing your tumor samples, your medical information, and your voice. Together, we can develop the most comprehensive understanding of this rare cancer.

If you have angiosarcoma, you have the power to make a difference in cancer research in a way that no one else can.

Your tumor and medical records could unlock discoveries.

The unique genetic information in your cancer could hold the key to rapid advances in cancer treatment. By looking at the DNA in your samples, researchers can make discoveries that will ultimately lead to a better understanding and faster advances in the treatment of angiosarcoma. This information will be invaluable to cancer research for years to come and could save many lives.

Become an integral part of this research movement.
Have a direct impact on the future.

By saying “Count Me In” you will partner with leading research institutes, hospitals, and patient advocacy groups by sharing part of your stored tumor tissue and copies of your medical records.

Here’s how you can participate

Step 1

Tell us about yourself

Click “Count Me In” and complete a simple online form to tell us about yourself and your cancer. We are asking all patients with angiosarcoma to say “Count Me In” and fill out the form so that we can learn more about your experiences with angiosarcoma.

Step 2

Give us permission to collect your samples and data

After you complete the online survey, we will ask you to fill out an online consent form that requests your permission to obtain copies of your medical records and some of your stored tumor tissue. We will do the rest — we’ll contact your doctors and hospitals to obtain copies of your medical records and a portion of your stored tumor samples. We’ll also send you a simple kit to collect a saliva sample.

Step 3

Learn with us along the way

We are excited to learn with you! Throughout the project, we will provide you with regular updates about the status of the project and share any discoveries that you have enabled us to make. We also may ask you additional questions about your experience to help with future studies — we want to hear what’s important to you.
